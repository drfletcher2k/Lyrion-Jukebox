# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two audiences: venue staff/admins on a permanent tablet kiosk, and guests on their own phones after scanning a QR code. The kiosk runs unattended in a loud, dimly-lit venue. Guests see the mobile page briefly — they scan, search, tap, done.

## Product Purpose

A song-request jukebox that lets venue guests add tracks to a shared Spotify-backed queue via their phones. The tablet kiosk displays what's playing and the queue, and lets staff manage track order and skip songs.

## Brand Personality

Premium, focused, alive. The interface should feel like a well-run venue — dark, confident, and in the moment.

## Anti-references

Generic music apps (Spotify's own UI is too safe). SaaS dashboards. Cream/beige "editorial" aesthetics. Anything that feels clinical or corporate.

## Design Principles

1. The room is always on — the kiosk is a live display, not a static screen. Everything should feel in-motion.
2. Guests are on unfamiliar hardware in a loud place — the mobile guest page must be instantly obvious, zero friction.
3. Depth through layers — dark surfaces gain richness from shadows, borders, and subtle glows, not flat color.
4. Precision over decoration — every visual element earns its place by clarifying hierarchy or affordance.
5. Tactile interactions — buttons and rows should feel physically responsive, not just color-swap on hover.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. All muted text must meet 4.5:1 contrast on dark surfaces. Focus rings on all interactive elements. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored globally.
